üìã Ingredients

Roasted Garlic:
4 Medium Whole Heads Of Garlic
1/2 Cup Olive Oil
Salt & Pepper
Focaccia:
5 Cups All-purpose Unbleached Flour
2 1/2 Teaspoons Instant Yeast
3 Tablespoons Extra Virgin Olive Oil
1 Teaspoon Sea Salt
2 Cups Warm Water
Topping:
Coarse Sea Salt
Extra Virgin Olive Oil

üë©‚Äçüç≥ Instructions

  1. Preheat oven to 425 degrees f.
  2. To prepare your garlic, first remove the outer skin of the heads of garlic, keeping the bulbs intact.
  3. Cut off the top 1/2 inch off the top of the bulbs.
  4. Place the bulbs in a small ovenproof bowl, and drizzle with the olive oil.
  5. Cover with a lid or aluminum foil, and bake in a 450 degree F. oven for around forty minutes, or until the garlic is soft.
  6. Let the garlic cool to room temperature.
  7. For the focaccia, measure and assemble your flour, oil, salt, yeast, and water.
  8. Squeeze the garlic into the focaccia dough, and reserve the oil for later.
  9. Add half the water and stir to mix.
  10. Continue to add water until the dough begins to come together into a shaggy ball.
  11. Dump the dough mixture onto a lightly floured surface and begin to knead with the heels of your hand. Knead for about 5 minutes, or until the dough is smooth and pliant.
  12. Add a little oil (2 tablespoons) to the bottom of a large bowl and place your ball of dough inside.
  13. Roll the ball around in the oil, ensuring the sides of the bowl, and ball of dough are both lightly oiled.
  14. Cover your bowl with plastic wrap and place in a warm spot to rise. (I cover mine with a kitchen towel on top of the plastic wrap and set it on a large sunny windowsill.)
  15. Let the dough rise until it is doubled in size, about an hour to an hour and a half depending on the ambient temperature.
  16. Preheat your oven to 425 degrees F.
  17. Grease a 13 x 9-inch sheet pan with olive oil.
  18. Use the tips of your fingers to dimple the entire top of the focaccia, and then drizzle with the reserved olive oil turning the pan carefully to allow the oil to roll into the indentations.
  19. Sprinkle coarse sea salt across the top of the focaccia.
  20. Bake the focaccia until golden brown, about 25 minutes, turning pans as needed during baking to it browns evenly.
  21. Cool 15 minutes before cutting into squares.
